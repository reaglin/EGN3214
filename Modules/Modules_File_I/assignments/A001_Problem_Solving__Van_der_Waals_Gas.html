Background

The Ideal Gas Law

The Ideal Gas Law assumes gas molecules have no volume and exert no intermolecular forces:

`

P = nRT / V

`

Where:

  • P = pressure (Pa)
  • n = amount of substance (mol)
  • R = universal gas constant = 8.314 J·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹
  • T = temperature (K)
  • V = volume (m³)

The Van der Waals Equation

The Van der Waals equation corrects the Ideal Gas Law for two physical realities:

  • Gas molecules have a finite volume (the volume correction, constant b)
  • Gas molecules attract one another (the pressure correction, constant a)

`

P = nRT / (V - nb) - a n² / V²

`

Where a and b are gas-specific constants. For this assignment, you are analyzing carbon dioxide (CO₂):

| Constant | Value | Units |

|---|---|---|

| a | 0.3658 | Pa·m⁶·mol⁻² |

| b | 4.286 × 10⁻⁵ | m³·mol⁻¹ |

> Note: These constants have known accepted (theoretical/literature) values. Your job is to use these accepted values to generate predicted pressures and compare them to the measured data.

Percent Error

Percent error quantifies how far a model's prediction is from the measured value:

`

percent_error = ((predicted - measured) / measured) * 100

`

A value near zero indicates good agreement. A positive value means the model over-predicts; a negative value means it under-predicts.

Measurement Error

No instrument reads perfectly. Assume the pressure sensor used to collect this data has a manufacturer-specified accuracy of ±1.5% of the measured value. This means any percent error within ±1.5% may be attributable to instrument error alone, not model error. You must address this in your conclusions.
